The 2025 AL MVP race

At the All-Star break, it has become clear that only two men have a serious chance at MVP in the American League: Aaron Judge and Cal Raleigh.

Judge is a right fielder for the New York Yankees while Raleigh is a catcher for the Seattle Mariners.

Aaron Judge's Case

Getty Images

God of WAR

According to FanGraphs, Judge leads MLB in WAR (Wins Above Replacement, a general stat that encompasses all aspects of a player). He has 7.4 WAR. Raleigh is 2nd, but has a noticeable drop-off with 6.2 WAR.

On Baseball Reference, Judge leads MLB with 7.1 WAR, while Raleigh is 3rd with 4.8 WAR.

    Judge leads MLB in
  • batting average
  • on-base percentage
  • slugging
  • and more advanced offensive stats like wRC+ and wOBA

    • Better Team?

    MVP is an individual award. In my opinion, making the postseason should not factor into a player's MVP chances at all. However, for a few voters, this is the case.

    Both the Yankees and Mariners are in wild card contention. The Yankees are only 2 games behind the Toronto Blue Jays, while the Mariners are 5 GB the Houston Astros. However, each team's Pythagorean W-L tell a different story. The Yankees are 53-43, but would expected to be 59-37. The Mariners are 51-45, same as their Pythagorean W-L record.

    Cal Raleigh's Case

    Getty Images

    Outta the Park

    Cal Raleigh leads MLB with 38 home runs. The Mariners have played 96 MLB games. Raleigh would have to hit 24 homers in 64 games to tie Judge's 2022 record of most AL homers in a single season. Raleigh is on pace for 64 home runs if he keeps up the same momentum as before the All-Star break.

    Voter Fatigue

    Aaron Judge won Rookie of the Year in 2017 and finished 2nd in the MVP race. He won MVP in 2022 and 2024. After a while, it gets dull seeing the same faces win MVP year after year.

    However, it should be mentioned that voter fatigue is less prominent in MLB than in other sports. Barry Bonds won MVP 7 times!
